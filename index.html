Anant Gupta

I work with Prof. Upamanyu Madhow in the WCSL lab at UCSB . My research aims at developing sensing and inference frameworks for low cost millimeter wave systems. I have worked in industry (Qualcomm, National Instruments ) on algorithm design and development projects for wireless technologies such as 5G mmW, 802.11x WLAN, NFC, GPS. My interest lie at streamlining signal processing frameworks for sensing in future autonomous applications. I received MS from UCSB in 2016 and BTech+MTech in ECE from IIT Kharagpur in 2013.

Research: mm-wave sensing

Exploiting geometric structure to solve data association problem effciently for multi-sensor localization.
Designed a Multi-objective optimization to construct large-effective aperture antennas using sparse array of subarray architectures for radar localization and 5G communication applications.
Investigated the localization accuracy achievable using Super-resolution algorithms for 2D FMCW radar model. Developed system level insights for array design strategies to optimize sensing coverage.
